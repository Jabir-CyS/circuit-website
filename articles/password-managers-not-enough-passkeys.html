Password managers aren't enough anymore. Here's what's next

Passkeys are finally seeing real adoption. A look at how the shift is playing out across major platforms.

NF Noor Fatima · 2026-07-02 · 3 min read

Passkeys — login credentials tied to your device instead of a typed password — have been available for a few years, but adoption is finally picking up as more major platforms make them the default option rather than an opt-in feature.

The appeal is straightforward: passkeys can't be phished the way passwords can, because there's no shared secret being typed into a page that could be fake. Even a well-crafted phishing site can't trick a passkey into authenticating on the wrong domain.

The remaining friction is cross-device use — passkeys created on one phone don't always sync smoothly to a laptop or another device. That gap is closing, but it's still the main reason many users stick with password managers as a backup, even as passkeys become more common.
