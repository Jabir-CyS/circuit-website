Five productivity apps actually worth paying for in 2026

Most productivity apps aren't worth a subscription. These five earned their place on our home screen.

BH Bilal Hassan · 2026-06-28 · 4 min read

The productivity app market is crowded, and most of it is forgettable — reskinned to-do lists with a subscription wall. A small number of apps, though, have earned a genuine place in daily workflows by solving one problem clearly instead of trying to do everything.

What stood out across the apps that made this list wasn't feature count — it was restraint. Each one does a narrow job well: one focuses purely on quick capture of notes with no folders or tags to manage, another handles recurring task scheduling with almost no setup required.

The common thread: the best productivity tools right now are the ones that get out of the way fastest, not the ones with the longest feature list.

Written by Bilal Hassan
