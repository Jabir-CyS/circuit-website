Why more founders are choosing to stay bootstrapped

With funding harder to close, a growing number of early-stage teams are deliberately avoiding VC money — and it's working for some.

BH Bilal Hassan · 2026-07-04 · 4 min read

As venture funding has tightened, a number of early-stage founders are opting out of the fundraising path altogether — building profitable, if slower-growing, businesses funded by customer revenue instead of investor capital.

The appeal is control. Bootstrapped founders don't answer to a board, don't need to hit growth targets set by someone else, and can make decisions on their own timeline. The trade-off is speed: without outside capital, growth is naturally capped by how much revenue the business can generate and reinvest.

It's not right for every business — capital-intensive ideas still need outside funding to get off the ground. But for software products with low overhead, bootstrapping is proving to be a realistic, and sometimes preferable, path.

Written by Bilal Hassan
