A fintech founder's honest take on raising a seed round in 2026

Investors want traction before conversation. One founder walks through what actually worked, and what wasted three months.

AF Ayesha Farooq · 2026-07-09 · 5 min read

Raising a seed round has quietly gotten harder over the past two years, and fintech founders are feeling it more than most. Investors that once funded a strong team and a compelling story now want to see real transaction volume before they'll take a second meeting.

One founder, who closed a modest seed round earlier this year after nearly six months of fundraising, shared what actually moved the needle: not the pitch deck, but a simple dashboard showing weekly transaction growth that she updated and shared with investors every Friday — whether they asked for it or not.

"The deck got me the first meeting. The weekly numbers got me the check," she said. She also warned against a common mistake: spending too much time perfecting a narrative before there's enough real usage data to back it up. In her experience, three months were lost pitching an idea that wasn't yet proven, time that could have gone into getting more users instead.
